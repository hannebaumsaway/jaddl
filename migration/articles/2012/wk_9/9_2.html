A complete one-eighty
Boom (who else?) shatters scoring record
In case you didn’t notice—and if you didn’t, get on fucking Twitter—The Mighty Boom beat the Fightin’ Longshanks 188.9–93.1 this past weekend.

188.9.

That’s not a typo. You know how you can tell? The number 180 is referenced in both the title of this article and the image above, that’s how.

As you probably guessed after reading the subtitle above, that mark is now the single-game high in JADDL history. By the time Sunday afternoon rolled around, it wasn’t a question of whether or not Boom would set the record; the only question was by how much.

The answer: 32.9 points. That’s right: if the previous highwater mark in all of JADDL play would have gone up head to head against Boom this weekend, it would have gotten smoked; burned; beaten by more than the average margin of victory for actual JADDL matchups (24.3).

188.9.

How do we even comprehend that number? If the ’Shanks would have joined forces in the East with either the Bensons, Millennium Falcons, or Odouls this past weekend, Boom still would have beaten them…

Longshanks + Bensons = 178.7
Longshanks + Falcons = 186.1
Longshanks + Odouls = 188.8 (what a game that would have been)

Only a combination with Football Jesus (198.8) would have put Boom away, and not by much.

Doug Martin was the primary catalyst for Boom on this historic occassion, exploding for 57.2 points. So let’s put that individual tally in context: Doug Martin, by himself, would have beaten the Bensons and IPP in Week 2, the Monkeys in Week 3, and Jesus in Week 7.

On the other side of the spectrum, Boom did this with zero points from its tight end, some guy named Kyle Rudolph. So Kyle Rudolph gets to go down in history as starting for the team that broke the 180 barrier without lifting a fucking finger. And with QB Matt Ryan scoring below his season average. A lucky TD and some garbage crossing routes could have gotten Boom to 200, but that’s a threshold that will have to wait. For now.

188.9.

The average score in 2012 so far is 92.7. Had Boom put up 92.7, Week 9 in 2012 would have ranked a pedestrian 26th all time in terms of total points. With 188.9, it ranks sixth. 188.9 is more than a whole extra team added to the scoreboard with league-average output. Think about that… take the league average, double it, and you’d still only be at 185.4.

Here’s another fun fact: no two scores from the Upset Underdogs’ 2007 campaign can be combined to match or exceed 188.9.

As a friendly reminder, I posed the following question back after Week 1: “Are any of you owners out there just a wee bit worried that…The Mighty Boom is going for total domination this year?” If you weren’t worried back then, you fucking should have been.

188.9.

Quite simply: holy shit.
