Far more entertaining than Battle: Los Angeles
The Battle of Phoenix goes Hauloll’s way
Welcome, boys and girls, to another season of JADDL fake football!

And damned if we didn’t start the year off with some fantastic games. Headlining the week was a rivalry game right out of the gate: the Red Hornets hopping on a cross-town bus to face off against their city limits nemesis, Team Hauloll. Hauloll came into the game 5-3 lifetime against the Ginger Stingers, and had won four in a row prior to losing in downright embarrassing fashion to the hapless Hornets a year ago, 103.4–64.5.

Hauloll wound up missing the postseason by two games anyway, so it didn’t really matter in the grand scheme of things; in fact, the loss spurred Hauloll to two 100+ games to finish out the season. Jason talked at length about the psychological impact that Hornets loss had on his team.
Just like my dad, the King of Marysville, always said: a properly loaded flatbed truck is a balanced one.
Jason

“It made us mad.”

Angering the winningest franchise in the JADDL probably isn’t the greatest thing that can happen to a team, especially when that franchise gets a chance at revenge so early the following season.

Well, Hauloll’s revenge was exacted, but it wasn’t easy. The Hornets—fresh off their B+ Guti Grade—didn’t back down an inch from the challenge, and forced Hauloll to scratch and clow all the way through Monday Night Football to finally secure a 111.6–99.2 victory. Jason’s squad was led by Andre Johnson with 17.9 points, but it was their balance that really carried the day—they had five players score 15 or more in all.

“Just like my dad, the King of Marysville, always said: a properly loaded flatbed truck is a balanced one,” said Jason following the game.

The Red Hornets were more top-heavy than balanced, on the other hand; the Dolly Parton of fantasy football. Following that analogy, Tony Romo and Stevan Ridley were the Hornets’ tits, putting up 25.4 and 21.2 respectively. And no, I don’t know who Stevan Ridley is. In that respect, could he be the new Arian Foster? You know, a runningback with a funny first name and normal last name who bursts onto the scene from nowhere to steal the hearts of fantasy footballers across the country? We’ll see.

In the meantime, Dan will have to console himself with Ridley’s apparent potential while swallowing a season-opening loss.

The game was still in doubt following Sunday’s action. Only when Hauloll posted an additional 15 points on Monday night via the Ravens D/ST to outpace the 4.9 by the Hornets’ Robert Meachem was the game decided. Prior to that point, the margin was minuscule—a 96.6–94.3 barnburner.

Now the doubt is squarely on the side of the Red Hornets for the time being, who surely remember going six weeks without a win to start the season a year ago. As for Hauloll, they can continue to focus on returning to the playoffs after last year’s first-ever postseason whiff.
