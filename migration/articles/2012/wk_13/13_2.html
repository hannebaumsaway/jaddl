R.I.P. Longshanks dynasty
No Curse to blame this year
The time of death for the Fightin’ Longshanks dynasty probably came and went a couple years ago, but 2012 sealed it beyond a shadow of a doubt. And to confirm the passing, we’re pushing a team feature to page 2 of the newsletter for the first time in newsletter history.

A year ago, the ’Shanks went 3-9-1. That’s not good. However, the real story was how they did so poorly. Namely: The Curse. It seemed that a supernatural funk had befallen the once proud franchise, causing them to lose in bizarre, improbable ways.

The 2011 ’Shanks set a record for the most points ever scored by a team which didn’t get a playoff bid. In JADDL history, only twice has a team been beaten multiple times in a single season by less than a point. In both instances, the victim was the ’Shanks.

2012 was a different story entirely—well, almost entirely.

The Curse-related piece first: the ’Shanks set a league record for most points scored against them, and by a considerable margin. The ’Shanks had 61 more points scored on them this year than any team in history. The median value for Points Against in JADDL play is 1,161.9…the ’Shanks had 1,410.8 dropped on them this season.
I wanted to believe 2011 was just a bad nightmare from which I’d wake up.
Ryan

So yes, the Longshanks weren’t done any favors, yet again. But that’s not the real story here. Not this time.

This year, the Longshanks just flat-out sucked. It didn’t matter what their opponents did. It didn’t matter whether The Curse lingered above Prairie Village for another round. The ’Shanks scored the 13th least points in a season since the JADDL was formed, and nothing its opponents could do would have salvaged this train wreck of a season.

“I wanted to believe 2011 was just a bad nightmare from which I’d wake up,” said Ryan. “Now I’m starting to think the years before 2009 were just a dream instead.”

At any rate, it’s getting harder and harder for Longshanks fans to recall what the playoffs are like, seeing as how their team of choice hasn’t been there in four years.

And at this point, the trend tells us things are only getting worse for this franchise as it loses its grasp on all fragments of its former glory.
