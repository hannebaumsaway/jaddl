Soaring Falcons win third straight
Millennium meets Jesus atop the Eastern mountain
Welcome back to the JADDL spotlight, Cam Newton!

The toast of the league a year ago after being snatched up off the waiver wire—well, you all know that story already—Newton had yet to really break out in 2012. A nice Week 2 (25.1 points) was bracketed by two pretty shitty weeks (12.4 and 10.2). Cammy Cam only scored that low or lower a single time all of last season. Through four weeks in 2011, Newton had nine touchdowns to five turnovers; this year he’s got seven TDs and six TOs. Both metrics moved in the wrong direction if you’re Ian Wolfe.

Prior to this past weekend, Newton had four TDs to those six TOs. In case you can’t handle the math, that means he put up three scores without any interceptions or fumbles. That’s progress in the right direction, as Ian will tell you.

“Of course I still see a potential star in Cameron, or I wouldn’t have drafted him in the first round, stupid.”

Easy, fella.

Newton (31.2) didn’t take care of the previously undefeated Lawrence Football Jesus by himself. He had plenty of help from—well, a lot of people, starting with the 49ers D/ST. The Niner Nasties posted a cool 28 points by shutting out the Jets while snagging eight turnovers, returning one of those for a touchdown, blocking a kick, and recording three sacks on the nutsack-less Mark Sanchez.

Three other Falcons passed the 15-point mark as well, including kicker Matt Prater.

The Jesus didn’t know what hit it.

“[Our score] would have beaten every other East team this week,” lamented James on The DDL earlier this week.

Unfortunately for James, his squad was playing the Millennium Falcons, and his score was good enough only to somewhat lessen the blow in a 144.5–98.6 bludgeoning.

The Falcons are now 3-1 alongside Jesus (yet still 49 points behind) and have a seemingly weak Team Odouls (1-3) to contend with before entering interdivisional play. Of course, Millennium’s only loss to date was to the other 1-3 team in the East, the Fightin’ Longshanks, so Ian would be wise to not look past Adam this weekend.

But for now, hats off to the Most Successful Expansion Franchise in JADDL History, for posting the ninth highest regular season score ever, for knocking the 900-pound gorilla in the East—Jesus…yes, that Jesus—down a peg, and for continuing to rock some insanely sexy uniforms.
