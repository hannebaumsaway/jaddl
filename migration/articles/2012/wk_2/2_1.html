Sniffing beers and flying high
IPA Connoisseur makes early statement in big win
In 2011, the One Pump Chumps put up 156. The year before that, In Pursuit of Perfection scored 154.6.

Why am I telling you this? Because aside from those two moments, no other team in JADDL regular season history has scored more than the IPA Connoisseur did in its 150.4–87.1 beatdown of the Red Hornets this past weekend.

That impressive outing has put IPA in as comfy a spot as a team can find itself after just two weeks: 2-0 and 76.2 points ahead of the also-undefeated Tulsa Angry Monkeys. It also gives braggart extraordinaire Lannie—the man who recently described his unremarkable franchise as “very deserving for such a privilege” as the number one overall pick in the JADDL draft—plenty to talk about.

“Of course I believe we have what it takes to win it all. Why wouldn’t we? We had the top pick, we made the playoffs last year, and we have me as a GM.”

Well, I don’t know about all that, but the Connoisseur did have plenty of firepower this weekend. Only one IPA player—the Chargers D/ST—failed to get into double figures (6). Four—four!—players scored in the 20s: Arian Foster (20.7), Philip Rivers (22.1), Victor Cruz (25.9), and Trent Richardson (26.5).
Of course I believe we have what it takes to win it all…We had the top pick, we made the playoffs last year, and we have me as a GM.
Lannie

But how sustainable is this?

Arian Foster is expected to put up sizeable numbers all season as the league’s top pick. Rivers? C’mon…we all know he’s going to struggle often, sometimes horribly. Cruz is the epitome of hot ’n’ cold; in 2011, Cruz scored over 15 five times, and only once did he follow up such a performance with another 15+ the next week. He scored under five three times as well, and put up just 5.8 last week.

Then again, who knows? Lannie will remind anyone who will listen that he led the league in scoring for nine weeks in 2011. IPA fans will remind him that he didn’t lead the league in scoring when it mattered most—in Week 13. He didn’t win the West Division. He didn’t win a playoff game.

He did talk a whole lot of shit in those nine weeks, though (Exhibit A | B | C | D | E | F). In all fairness, he opened the door to this very mockery before the end: “I will graciously accept any and all backlash and ridicule from my comments thus far if I happen to end this season sucking.” The Connoisseur losing four of their last five certainly qualifies as ending the season sucking.

As for the poor Hornets, they appear here for the second straight week on the wrong side of the feature. So, with their franchise sitting at 0-2, how prevalent is the painful memory of the 0-6 start from a year ago in the minds of Hornets fans?

Once they wake up, we’ll ask them.
