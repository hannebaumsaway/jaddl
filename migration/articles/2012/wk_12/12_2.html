The unfortunate Mr. 11
How the 11th draft slot has taken a dump on the league for years
It’s possible this could be a chicken-or-the-egg situation. It’s possible the sample size still isn’t large enough to draw any concrete conclusions.

One thing is for certain, though: the 11th draft position is a statistical hellhole.

Let’s examine the slots in terms of regular season wins their teams have produced over the years. The top dog? Pick #3, which has produced 49 wins to date. Go down the list and you get to the second worst slot, which happens to be Pick #12, with 31 wins.

So, there are 18 wins separating first place and eleventh place. To get down to last place—Pick #11, of course—you have to keep going down another 10 wins (to 21 wins).

Assuming the Fightin’ Longshanks lose next week—meaning Pick #11 would still be at 21 wins—Pick #11 would average 3.5 wins per season if it were an actual team. Awesome.

In terms of winning percentages, the second-to-last slot—still Pick #12—produces teams that win 41% of their games. Pick #11 clocks in at 29%. In more stark terms, teams who drafted 11th have lost 70% of their games (there were two ties in there as well).

Here’s where the picture gets a bit cloudier for Pick #11: points. Pick #11 has produced more points (during the regular season) than three other slots—#8, #12, and #10.

How can this be? Pick #11 is so atrocious in terms of wins compared to the other slots, how on earth are they not dead last in points as well?

This is where the “unfortunate” part of the article title comes in. Teams who drafted 11th have been scored against more than teams who drafted in any other position. Why? Who knows? Maybe Jared Allen hates the number 11. Again, maybe the sample size is too small yet.

Or maybe it’s a somewhat self-fulfilling prophecy due to the fact that, by chance, general managers who were going to have a shitty draft just happened to draw the #11 pick. Then, when they inevitably drafted shittily, Pick #11 was caught holding the bag, for lack of a better phrase. It doesn’t help that poor Pick #11 drew the now dreadful Fightin’ Longshanks the past two years, years in which the ’Shanks rewarded Pick #11 with four total wins.

Then again, maybe the ’Shanks are now dreadful because they got saddled with the 11th pick twice in a row…’Round and ’round we go!

Let’s take one more shot at trying to address the chicken-or-egg proposition. Of the teams that have drafted 11th at one time or another—The Mighty Boom, Red Hornets, Lawrence Football Jesus, Team Odouls, and of course the ’Shanks—we’ve established that they’ve collectively gone 21-54-2 in the combined years when they actually drafted 11th. But how did those same teams do in the years when they didn’t draft 11th?

172-134-4. A .561 winning percentage, as opposed to the .286 average Pick #11 is batting to date. Interesting contrast, wouldn’t you say?

Oh, and in case you’re wondering why I didn’t produce any playoff stats for Pick #11, that’s because there aren’t any. That’s right: no team which picked 11th has ever made the postseason. That includes the mighty Mighty Boom, remember.

That’s right: Boom once missed the playoffs. It was 2007, and they drafted 11th that year. They ended up going 3-9-1 and stayed home for the holidays.

Since then? Boom has avoided Pick #11, and they’ve gone 46-18 to this point, with a playoff spot already clinched for 2012.

Yeah…whether it’s the chicken or the egg, the cart or the horse, Pick #11 fucking sucks.
