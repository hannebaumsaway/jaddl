Millennium Falcons 107.5  |  Fightin' Longshanks 105.5
Hey, look, another team scoring well above its average against the Fightin’ Longshanks! Longshanks? More like Longshots, at this point. The IPA Connoisseur 126.0  |  In Pursuit of Perfection 68.1
Not a shining moment for the reigning champs. Getting 2.2 points from a healthy quarterback is hilarious, though I doubt Nolan found it very funny. Calvin “I have a big” Johnson and the Redskins D/ST were the only two double-digit scorers for IPP. IPA, on the other hand, vaulted to the top of the league power rankings with a solid effort led by a crushing 68.6-point output from its three-headed RB corps. That’s right…IPA’s RBs alone outscored Pursuit’s entire squad.


The Mighty Boom 75.7  |  The Lawrence Football Jesus 108.3

Vick went for 30, so The Jesus went for a victory. We can’t really judge Boom until he gets Miles Austin and Aaron Hernandez (I mean it!) back in the starting lineup.

Bad News Bensons 83.5  |  Team Odouls 70.5
The bad news for Bad News: they had a pretty disappointing week. The good news for Bad News: they continue to be blessed with horrible outings from opponents, keeping their lossless streak alive. This is the second time already that the Bensons’ opponent has scored under 80, and he hasn’t even played the Red Hornets! As for Odouls, QB Joe Flacco took a look at Jay Cutler’s 2.2 points for IPP and said, “I can top that!” And he did, in a manner of speaking, submarining the Near Beers with a 2.1 tally.
