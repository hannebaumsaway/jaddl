A thirst for the worst
contributed by James
It’s time to get serious about the consolation bracket. That’s why I’ve devised the Anti-Championship for the six-teams who didn’t make the JADDL Playoffs. The idea isn’t to find out who’s the best of the worst, it’s to find out who’s the worst of the worst. I’m sure the Commish would want me to point out at this time that the Anti-Championship is not in anyway sanctioned by the JADDL higher-ups. At least not yet. Also, I will accept bids on the name of this thing. The Anti-Championship is too “just the facts” for the name of such an awesome tourney. The Bad News Alexes have suggested the “Short-Bus Tournament.” I think it should be called “The DC Football Obama Memorial Classic sponsored by Herman Cain.”

The idea is simple, the DCFOMC is like a mirror of the actual JADDL Playoffs. You don’t advance by winning, you advance by losing. The best teams don’t get byes, the worst teams do. The ultimate “winner” is the ultimate “loser.” Only one team can lose their way to dead fucking last, so let’s play it out elimination style!

Since the matchups will bear out differently than the “consolation ladder” thing ESPN has crafted, I’ve devised a bracket to track how the Anti-Championship is playing out. (NOTE: I’ve been out-of-the playoffs more than my share of years, and I have yet to figure out exactly what this “consolation ladder” determines: it’s like some sort of round-robin tourney into nothingness.) You’ll notice that the worst teams get home-field advantage, but I’m not sure if it should be the other-way around. Anyways, after an exciting Wildcard Round, Adam Jones’s Near Beers and Ron Prince’s Shell Corporation have earned the opportunity to lose to the worst team in each of their respective divisions, the disgraced Red Hornets and Fightin’ Longshanks.
