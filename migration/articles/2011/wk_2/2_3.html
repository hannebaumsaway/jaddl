Lockout fails to slow down the JADDL
In the words of Millennium Falcons receiver Chad Ochocinco:

Fuckin’ wow.

Scoring records are being shattered left and right in this young season. Clearly, JADDL players didn’t jack around during their vacation prolonged by the NFL lockout. And it’s not just the skill position players, either. Oft-unsung D/STs are putting up points in bunches as well. It’s as if the league as a whole was infused with Cammy Cam juice, and they likey.

League-wide, the previous high mark for total points scored through Week 2—2,394 in 2007—was eclipsed by the 2,429.6 tally put up this year.

That charge was led by—who else?—The Mighty Boom, which utterly destroyed the individual team Week 2 record total with 263.5. The former occupant of the top spot was also Boom (again, who the fuck else would it be?) with 249.1 in ’09.

As you may have noticed—or would have heard if you follow @JADDL_Commish on Twitter—that 249.1 score was eclipsed this year not only by Boom, but by The IPA Connoisseur as well. In other words, 2011 now occupies the top two spots on this completely irrelevant page of the record books.

“It’s fantastic,” said JADDL Commissioner Ryan. “It just shows how much these individuals live for fantasy football. So really, it’s pretty sad. But…still fantastic. Fantastically sad.”

Sad?

Child, please.
