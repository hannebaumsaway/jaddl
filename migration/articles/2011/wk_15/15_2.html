The semis are over and Boom is still standing
Three straight playoff appearances. Three straight semifinal exits.

But this past weekend, The Mighty Boom ensured it won’t be four straight. A 119.4–102.3 victory over the East champion Bad News Bensons took care of that.

The Bensons got 36.8 points from Drew Brees, but he and the Falcons D/ST were the only two players to eclipse 15. 102.3 is a fine score, but it wasn’t enough to derail the Boom train, which scored over 115 for the fourth consecutive week.

Tom Brady led the way once again for The Mighty with 27, followed closely LeSean McCoy (25.7) and Aaron Hernandez (20.5). Also—and this is a big damn deal for Boom—they got a positive contribution from their D/ST slot.

Peter expressed his feelings about the D/ST role in fantasy football during the game via Twitter: “Ugh. I freaking hate defenses.”

You know who he doesn’t hate? Gisele Bundchen’s boy toy. The Patriots got their shit together, and Brady is Brady again all of a sudden. That, along with the continued dominance of LeSean McCoy, means the Boomers are bad motha fuckas once again.

The Mighty’s conquest means the Bensons have been denied a return trip to the Jared Bowl. After holding off the Millennium Falcons to keep hold of the Eastern Goblet, Bad News couldn’t keep the momentum going into the postseason.

Let’s face it: a team starting John Kuhn at running back didn’t deserve to make it.

“It was a good run. I did it to myself. @drewbrees deserved better,” Alex tweeted following the loss.

Now we find out who takes home the Court-Ordered Limousine. Will it be the Virtual Vermeil winner in the Chumps, or will it be the new hotness in the JADDL, Boom?

Tune in to find out. Jared Bowl V: same bat time, same bat channel.
