Con·nois·seur: n. One who enjoys rare things…such as wins, in this case
It’s been quite awhile since The IPA Connoisseur has tasted success along with a sumptuous India Pale Ale. With the lack of action in the win column, a more appropriate moniker would be India Stale Ale. In fact, IPA hasn’t had a winning record since Week 11…of 2009. To put this into perspective, that was November 23rd of that year.

660 days ago.

For fuck’s sake, Lannie, we know you got married and had a kid in that timeframe, but get your damn priorities straight.

And so he has. For one week, at least. You can’t do much better to start a new season than breaking your franchise record for points scored in a game. Not only did The Connoisseur break its record; it broke it off in Team Hauloll’s ass, 134.3–85.8. The Connoisseur was 1-4 against Hauloll coming into the game.

Lannie says he tried to reach outward for help on improving his woeful franchise during the long lockout-plagued offseason, but ended up looking inward instead due to lack of available assistance.

“I went to Barnes & Noble to peruse their self-help section, but all I could find was a book called Good to Great,” said Lannie. “So I researched online, and sure enough, there isn’t even a book out there called Shitty to Good. So I did some introspection instead, and said ‘Lannie, quit sucking ass!’”

So far so good for the advice from the proverbial man in the mirror. Lannie’s meditation led him to believe Matt Hasselbeck could actually get Kenny Britt the ball enough to make him a viable fantasy receiver. Whaddayaknow: five catches, 136 yards, and an 80-yard TD. That equates to 27.6 fantasy points and, combined with Ray Rice’s 26.9 and Matt Forte’s 23.8, a dominating win over the defending West Division champs.

And it could have been much worse. Hauloll isn’t getting 30 points from Wes Welker on a weekly basis (insert compulsory laughter at the Dolphins’ secondary). They might get less than 10 points per game from in-danger-of-being-a-has-been DeAngelo Williams and I-don’t-do-training-camps Chris Johnson for the next few weeks, however, and that could put them in a big hole.
All I could find was a book called Good to Great.…There isn’t even a book out there called Shitty to Good.
Lannie, IPA Connoisseur owner

Then again, maybe if kicker Nate Kaeding didn’t get hurt at the outset of his game (ha, what?), he could have put up 49 points and we’d be lauding Hauloll’s continued dominance. Or not. Either way, Jason was in full rationalization mode well before the final whistle.

“I actually prefer my main guys all have their bad games at the same time if my opponent is going to go off,” Jason said in a Gmail Chat conversation with the league commissioner during Monday night’s games.

An opponent going off has never stopped you from winning in the past, Jason. Getting soft?

In any case, it’s been a year, nine months, and 22 days since The Connoisseur could feel this good about themselves, and the League likes nothing more than a feel-good story. Right, Michael Vick? (Ooo, sorry Alex…too soon?)
