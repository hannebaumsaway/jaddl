Haul Day
As in, Adrian “Haul Day” Peterson
When the hour of the season is late, and you’re sitting at .500, games against division rivals which also sit at .500 are amplified measurably.

Win, and deal your key playoff competitor a two-game blow. Lose, and they do the same to you. And as the season draws to a close, opportunities to make deficits up become as precious—and scarce—as a well-executed mullet.

That was the situation facing Team Hauloll this past weekend; 4-4, facing off against fellow 4-4 Westerner Tulsa. The IPA Connoisseur all but has a postseason spot secured at 7-2, but the other two spots in the West are up for grabs. In Pursuit of Perfection is 6-3 and in second at the moment. Throw in the Monkeys and Hauloll, and that makes three teams vying for two available spots in the Big Show.
Since when does deserving a playoff spot have anything to do with getting one?
Jason

So yeah … when you get a square shot at one of those teams, you have to take advantage.

And take advantage Hauloll did.

Chris “CJ1K” Johnson (he ain’t getting to 2K this year) led them to a 131.4–70.9 dismantling of the Monkeys with 24 points. Adrian Peterson and Russell Wilson pitched in with 24 and 22, respectively.

This win ends a two-game skid for Hauloll—two weeks of getting rained on by the East—just in the nick of time. They also broke 100 for the first time since Week 4, and for just the third time overall.

“We hadn’t been playing well enough to deserve to advance, obviously” said Jason. “But since when does deserving a playoff spot have anything to do with getting one?”

Since never. That’s why, despite Hauloll’s lackluster play before this week, they find themselves controlling their own destiny from this point onward. With games coming up against the Red Hornets (2-7) and One Pump Chumps (1-8), Hauloll could … should … be 7-4 when it faces off against the West’s front runner, IPA. The Monkeys have to face both IPA and IPP in that three-week stretch, so they’ll have to bring their A-game to not be completely out of the hunt by the time they get the Hornets to close out the regular season.

But Hauloll can’t sit back and count on a Monkeys collapse. As the Hornets proved this past weekend when they came away with an improbable win over IPA, fantasy matchups aren’t played on paper. Well … I mean, they are played on paper, but … oh, you know what I mean.
