It’s just one week, right?
What Week 1 really means
This week, the losers of this past weekend’s games have been consoling themselves with the mantra, “It’s just one week,” or, “It’s a long season, guys!”

On the flip side, the winners have been tempering their own expectations in a similar fashion, reminding their giddy fans that one week does not a season make.

Or does it?

Let’s take a look at what Week 1 has meant for its winners and losers, historically. Winners first!

Over the past six seasons, 36 teams have emerged from the opening weekend victorious. Of those 36 teams, 26 went on to make the playoffs—eight made the quarterfinals, 11 the semis, and seven marched all the way to the Jared Bowl.

So, that’s a 72% clip for Week 1 victors earning postseason berths.

For the losers, the picture is much grimmer, as you would expect. In fact, if you remember any algebra from high school, you already know the numbers for the losers…they’re the exact opposite.

Just 28% of the Week 1 losers ended up making the playoffs—10 teams. If you’re looking for a ray of hope, losers, five of those 10 teams did make it all the way to the Jared Bowl. So, you know…keep fighting, and stuff.

And now a note for James in particular: there were two teams that tied in the opening weekend of the 2007 season…neither made the playoffs. So we’ll go ahead and estimate the chances for a team who ties at a firm 0%.

On a side note, defending champions are 4-2-1 in the opening weekend following their titles. What does that mean? Absolutely nothing. But I looked it up, so I figured I may as well share it.
