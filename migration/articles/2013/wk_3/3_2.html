Shanks issued warning
Mountain Time off the hook
In a shocking development this past weekend, a league general manager neglected to bench a player on his roster who was officially ruled out for his coming game. This is the first such instance since Red Hornets owner/GM Dan Romans was cited back in 2011.

The controversy this year centered around Reggie Bush being activated despite the Lions announcing Sunday morning that Bush would not be available. This time around, the Fightin’ Longshanks front office was the culprit, a fact which makes the league’s warning a bit of an awkward situation, considering the Longshanks’…uh…close association with the league office.

Nonetheless, a warning was indeed given, according to an offcial statement from the desk of the commissioner on Tuesday morning:

We can confirm that the Fightin’ Longshanks franchise has been cited for unacceptable negligence and failure to uphold the standards of the Jared Allen’s Designated Drivers League. Because this is a first offense, no further action will be taken against the franchise at this time. However, future violations will result in repercussions, likely monetary and/or humiliating in nature.

The league may wait for another offense to humiliate the Longshanks, but peer front offices are not.

On Sunday morning, The Mighty Boom’s people tweeted, “Its amateur hour over @FightinShanks. Bush? Really?”

More tweets were quickly added to the pile. “Is he throwing the game?” from The Bad News Bensons front office. “He was not going to play, it was common knowledge. You are exactly what I needed” from that week’s opponent, Lawrence Football Jesus. “Mountain time like” from In Pursuit of Perfection out of the West. And more.

Then came salvos from The DDL.

There was a satircal chart of Longshanks losses in relation to the use of Reddit for fantasy football news. There were screenshots. And more screenshots.

So what explanation did the Longshanks organization have for this fuck-up of epic* proportions?

“…been painting Babyshanks’ room all day with the father-in-law, so fuck right off” read the tweet from ’Shanks GM Ryan Hannebaum, when he was alerted to the snafu on Sunday.

Where are your priorities? cried Longshanks fans everywhere. Are we not family too? Do you care nothing for us?

The Longshanks’ front office did issue an apology on Tuesday morning, but actions speak louder than words. “I have let Longshanks Nation down. I can be better. I will be better” were the words.

Now let’s see the actions.

* “epic” because the roster mismanagement actually cost the Longshanks the game
