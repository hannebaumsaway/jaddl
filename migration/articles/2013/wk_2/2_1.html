Sting early, sting hard
Hornets don’t wait 8 weeks to win this year
A year ago, the Red Hornets lost seven straight games to open the season before finally winning one in Week 8. And let’s be honest: they only won in Week 8 because they got to play the Fightin’ Longshanks.

Well, 2013 is a new year, and the Hornets—like the ’Shanks the week before—announced loudly that they plan on things going differently this time around, in the form of a 151.8–94.4 thrashing of the One Pump Chumps (0-2).
We’re very tuned in to the ticking of the clock and flipping of the calendar here in the Mountain Time Zone.
Dan

This burst from the Hornets nearly matched the total they put up last season in weeks 5 and 6 combined (152.1). The year before that, the Hornets scored less than 152 combined points over a two-week stretch on four separate occasions. Needless to say, this franchise has nowhere to go but up from its recent history.

“2011 was 2011, last year was last year, and this year is this year,” said Dan following the game. “We’re very tuned in to the ticking of the clock and flipping of the calendar here in the Mountain Time Zone, and that awareness of past events being just that—past events that don’t dictate the future—is what I try to instill in my players each and every week.”

…You didn’t really think we were going to have a Red Hornets article without a Mountain Time mention, did you? Sorry, Dan.

Since we’re on that subject, it’s worth noting that since the league-issued warning after the first week of 2011, the Hornets’ front office has successfully avoided activating any players who had been ruled out for a given week’s games. Well done, Dan. (That just goes to show you all the power the JADDL league office wields.)

Now, back to this stellar performance by the ’Nets in Week 2.

Marshawn “Skittles” Lynch topped the charts with 31.5 points, but the game was well in hand before his Monday Night Football frenzy. All non-kickers for the Hornets got into double figures, with notably impressive outings from Julio Jones (26.2) and Jordy Nelson (18.6), as well as a surprising 16 points from the Patriots D/ST.

What brought on this encouraging onslaught from a squad that went 2-11 a year ago? “I had to bring in Bobby Knight for a pregame speech to light a fire under their asses,” Dan admitted.

In fact, the Hornets breached the one-fifty plateau despite getting an uninspired 11.2 points from QB Tom Brady. And, despite the easy victory for his team, Brady was visibly agitated on the sidelines during the game, reportedly yelling “Come on, leave Miley alone!” When asked about the incident afterward, Brady simply said, “Bitches be crazy, you know that.”

Well, one thing we do know is that the Hornets (1-1) have outscored the rest of the West through two weeks, and that’s a welcome change of pace for Hornets fans desperate to see its franchise get back its former glory.

Depth may be a concern, however. The Hornets’ bench put up just 21.9 points total, and 12.6 of those came from its backup QB, Big Ben Whataburger. Can a team which has to start either Rashard Mendenhall or Montee Ball each week do serious damage over the long term? We’ll have to wait for the answer.

(The answer is no.)
