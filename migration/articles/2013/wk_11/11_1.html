Regressing, but still mean
Jesus loses playoffs, gains respect?
2012 was a high mark for the Lawrence Football Jesus franchise. The most wins for them in JADDL play (9); the most points (1,251.4); a playoff berth.

Unless Jesus averages 167 points over these final two games, 2013 won’t boast any of those things.

But that’s okay! 2013 legitimizes Jesus in a different way. A way that says, Hey, our bad years now aren’t that bad anymore. Jesus could still score the second or third most points in franchise history this year, and could feasibly finish with a 6-7 record and once again out of the East’s cellar (they haven’t finished in last place since 2010).

For a franchise that finished last or next to last in three of its first four years in the league, having down years that are just mediocre instead of awful is a good feeling.
There was a time when other owners would pat me on the back for winning four games in a season.
James

Consistency is a key going forward for the Pigskin Prophets. Avoiding those tragic games that are remembered for all the wrong reasons, and having the ability to hit a big game against any opponent in any given week. In 2007, Jesus scored less than 60 six times. They did it twice in both 2010 and 2011. This year? None, so far.

The most times Jesus has scored 100+ in a single season is four, which they did in 2009 and 2012. They’ve already accomplished the feat four times this year, so they’ll have two chances to raise that particular bar. One of the ones already in the bag was this past weekend's 101.9–86.4 win over the Fightin’ Longshanks, completing a sweep of that flailing franchise for the third straight season, beating them for the sixth straight time overall. This follows the Shanks’ own six-game winning streak over Jesus in the first four years of JADDL play. Turnabout clearly is fair play.

James is pleased with the increasing respect his squad is receiving from his peers. “There was a time when other owners would pat me on the back for winning four games in a season. This year, I’m getting sympathetic looks and comments about next year. That psychological shift is invaluable to me.”

Another thing that may be invaluable to Jesus is momentum. If they can win their final two games—which would be no small feat, considering they play the Bensons (7-4) and Odouls (6-5)—they would carry a four-game winning streak into 2014, and could finish fourth in their division … though fifth seems all but locked in at this point.

Either way, this is a season a franchise can build on. It isn’t flashy, and it won’t be spoken of fondly by LFJ fans for years to come. But a 1- or 2-win season would have been, for all the wrong reasons. It could have been disastrous, destroying everything positive that was gained the past two seasons.

Disaster: averted.
