Familiarity breeds contempt
Another chapter in the hidden rivalry
You won’t find ESPN coating SportsCenter with wall-to-wall highlights of this game, annually.

It doesn’t usually set fans of either franchise all a-titter on Twitter in the days leading up to the main event.

In the league-wide survey distributed to front offices in 2011, neither owner considered the other a rival with any sort of vitriol.

But make no mistake: this is a rivalry.

Why? Because crazy shit always seems to happen in this game, that’s why.

I’m talking, of course, about The Lawrence Football Jesus versus Team Odouls. It’s the rivalry in the East you rarely hear about. But you really should make it a point to follow it. Because, like I said: crazy shit happens in these matchups.

In 2010, it took the insane combination of a two-point conversion in the final two minutes of regulation, a coin toss, and a game-winning field goal in overtime for Jesus to erase a 10-point Monday night deficit to edge Odouls.

And that was a revenge game.

The year before, Jesus was victimized by those fun little mid-week scoring adjustments ESPN sometimes likes to pull.

When the dust had settled on their 2009 battle, Jesus fans rejoiced at a victory over Team Odouls … a victory by nine tenths of a point.

Tuesday morning brought something other than joy in Lawrence, however. Overnight, ESPN decided Cardinals runningback Tim Hightower – remember that guy? – deserved one more point than he was originally given. Odouls owned Hightower at the time. Thus, Odouls ended up the victor by the smallest margin possible: a tenth of a point.

Later, another adjustment gave Odouls yet another point, meaning the game is in the books forever as a 1.1-point win for Odouls. But the legend will live on regardless.

In 2011, the second Jesus/Odouls game of that year can be summed up by a single NFL game: the Chiefs versus the Dolphins. The Chiefs were drilled in the contest, 31–3. Both the 31 and the 3 were supremely significant to Jesus and Odouls. The 3 was a Ryan Succop field goal, which gave Odouls four points. The 31 from Miami whittled the Chiefs D/ST score to -4 … and naturally, Jesus started the Chiefs D/ST that week. Without the Chiefs/Dolphins game, Jesus has itself a five-point victory. With it, Odouls won by three.

I swear to god I’m not making this up. Look it up, see for yourselves.

Now, in 2013, we have another story for the darkening saga of Odouls v. Jesus.

Think of this one as a kind of mix between the comeback heroics seen in the 2010 affair and the scoring adjustment nightmare of 2009.

Jesus went into Monday Night Football once again needing some magic from involved players to overcome a significant deficit. And magic they got. The Falcons – a team who suddenly can’t run the ball for shit – called Jacquizz Rodgers’ number in the red zone twice, and he delivered two touchdowns for the Football Jesus. Tony Gonzalez didn’t reach paydirt, but he did rack up 97 yards and 9.7 points of his own. Bilal Powell pitched in a measly 4.7, but in all, Jesus had itself 32.2 shiny new points to work with.

And with about 1:30 left in the game, Jesus suddenly had a lead. A lead by 2.3, but a lead nonetheless. A long climb, a hard journey, and a triumphant finish. Right?

That’s where the fairy tale ends for ol’ Jesus. Old Testament type brutality took over in the form of a game-winning drive by the New York fucking Jets. The resultant J-E-T-S Jets! Jets! Jets! field goal dropped the Falcons D/ST score from 3 … to zero.

… Which dropped Jesus’ score to 0.7 behind Odouls.

There’s still a chance ESPN could pull its adjustment shenanigans once again, this time to the benefit of LFJ. But it’s not likely.

What is likely is that we’re going to continue to get crazy outcomes from Odouls/Jesus games for years to come.

So when this game comes back around on the calendar, pay attention.
