PURSUIT OVER: PERFECTION FOUND
As they say, hindsight is twenty-twenty. In Pursuit of Perfection was the feature team in the Week 3 newsletter, and boy, are there some retrospective gems in that sucker &hellip

Success of any kind is a rarity [to Nolan’s franchise] …

… Nomac is nothing if not consistent…ly bad …

… perhaps the good people of Ta-Town should put the parade on hold …

Well, Wichitans, you can officially resume parade preparations. Your Perfectionists are champs of the JADDL after taking out The Bad News Bensons in Jared Bowl IV, 99.3 - 90.3.

There’s another nugget from that Week 3 newsletter that is less foot-in-mouth and more Nostradamus: … maybe Dwayne Bowe will start putting up decent numbers once Matt Cassel figures out how to be more like Tom Brady and less like Elvis Grbac. Well, lo and behold, Cassel had a Pro Bowl-caliber year, and the primary benefactor from that development was Dwayne Bowe. Bowe, and In Pursuit of Perfection. When the dust settled on the regular season, Bowe wasn’t just decent; he was the top receiver in the JADDL.

Bowe’s emergence moved IPP from playoff contender to title contender. With Michael Vick, Chris Johnson, and Darren McFadden in place, the Perfectionists were poised to make a run.

And make a run they did.

IPP closed out the regular season with a 3-1 sprint, averaging 115 points over that four-game stretch. When the playoffs arrived, they didn’t let up … in fact, they stepped it up a notch, averaging 127 points in the first two rounds, both lopsided victories over West Division rivals One Pump Chumps and Team Hauloll.

Then it was on to the Jared Bowl to take on the East Division Champion Bensons for the right to take home the Court-Ordered Limousine trophy. The Bensons were coming off a bye and a convincing victory over The Mighty Boom, so the matchup promised to be a good one.

It did not disappoint.

However, instead of the high scoring affair most expected, both teams were held under 100 points for the first time in their respective playoff runs. For the Bensons, Drew Brees had a mundane performance and Michael Turner was a no-show. For IPP, Vick had a somewhat tempered outing by his lofty standards, and Johnson and McFadden failed to pick up the slack.

But fantasy football is a team imaginary game, and IPP got help from the unlikeliest of sources when it needed it most …

LeGarrette Blount.

You may remember Blount from his (limited) days with the Oregon Ducks, punctuated—and cut short—by his unusual post-game meeting with a Boise State player in 2009. Well, his latest foray into national news is a more positive one; his 16.7 points put IPP on top of the fantasy football world.

This was Pursuit’s first jaunt into the JADDL playoffs, and they made it count. The Bensons treat the playoffs like an annual holiday retreat, but once again they go home empty-handed.

In a year that kicked off a new decade, the JADDL has a new franchise to add to its list of champions.

Savor it, Nolan.
