Odouls not out of runner-up race just yet, folks
It was a bleak scene in Houston going into Week 7. Bleak even by Houston’s standards. Beloved Team Odouls sat at 1-5 and had to travel to Manhattan to take on the 4-2 One Pump Chumps from the West Division.

Fans of the Big O were losing hope for another coveted second-place finish.

Then something frickin’ amazing happened: Thomas Jones scored 18.5 points on Sunday. Then something even amazing-er happened: Todd Heap scored 17.9 points of his own. Then the amazing-est thing happened: Chumps QB Tony Romo went down like the I.Q. of someone having a conversation with his former fling Jessica Simpson, breaking his left clavicle and arresting his fantasy output at a meager 5.4 points.

The result of all these amazing happenings? A 99.5 - 82.1 victory for the feisty Near-Beers.

“Couldn’t be more proud of our guys,” gushed Odouls owner Adam after the game. “They understand how much those silver Surrendered Keys mean to this franchise, and they went out and did what needed to be done.”

…Wait: did what needed to be done? A keen young reporter reminded Adam that his team was still just 2-5 on the season, four games back of The Mighty Boom, and tied for last place in the East.

“Shut the hell up,” Adam replied.

2-5 is a whole lot better than 1-6, however, and Adam has a right to remain hopeful for a playoff berth. Or does he? Maybe not. The worst record after seven weeks which produced a playoff team was 3-4 (Team Hauloll in 2008). Good news for The Flying Pigs and the Fightin’ Longshanks; not so good news for Odouls.

Oh well. It’ll all play itself out soon enough. For now, Adam can revel in the fact that at the very least, he was part of the East Division’s collective effort to even the odds in the interdivisional war, unlike the other 2-5 team, Football Jesus. Last week the hero, this week the goat. That is life in the JADDL.
