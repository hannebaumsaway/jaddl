The East (other than Jesus) strikes back
Well, that celebration was short-lived for the West Division.

Just a week after amassing a 5-1 lead in the interdivisional war, owners in the West watched in horror as that lead evaporated completely, leaving in its place a brand new ballgame.

Last week the West was 15.5 points away from a clean sweep of East squads to open the three-game cross-country battle. Only a Football Jesus victory over The IPA Connoisseur put a halt to the broom. In an ironic twist only the JADDL can properly produce, it was those same Gridiron Holy Ghosts who stopped the broom once again…from sweeping in the other direction. A 23.8-point loss to the Red Hornets kept the East from perfection, relegated instead to matching the West’s 5-1 mark from the previous week.

So here we stand, going into the final week of interdivisional play with the two divisions deadlocked at six wins apiece. Screw TNT; the JADDL knows drama.

In terms of points, the East outscored the West 545.0 to 513.2, moving the total interdivisional tally to 1083.8 - 1027.9, still in the West’s favor. The season total, however, is still weighted to the East, 3779.8 - 3656.4. A bunch of numbers there, all of which are completely meaningless. That’s how the Newsletter rolls.

If you recall, another quirk about Week 7 was the fact that every game was played in a West stadium. Just goes to show you that home field advantage means dick in fantasy football, eh? (Seriously, it means nothing in the JADDL, statistically; the commish made sure of that.)

Also just like a week ago, some of the scores are real head-scratchers. 1-5 Team Odouls dispatched the 4-2 One Pump Chumps by 17. The 2-4 Fightin’ Longshanks rallied on Monday night from 30 points down to beat 5-1 Team Hauloll by nine. And The Bad News Bensons held on for dear life on that same Monday, surviving a zero-point outing from Roy Williams and escaping with a one-point win over In Pursuit of Perfection, LLC. Here’s some salt in the wound for Nolan: Brandon Jacobs had a completely meaningless fumble while trying to run out the clock for the Giants, which of course cost IPP two points…and victory.

Not all the games had bizarre outcomes, though. The Millennium Falcons took care of business against the hapless IPA Connoisseur, 106.9 - 90.5. Sometimes bad things happen to bad teams.

One week of interdivisional play (and James’ wet dream) remains, and thanks to this week’s vicious retaliation, the East owners may yet be able to back up all the shit they talked during the preseason.
