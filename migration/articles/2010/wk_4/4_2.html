Pigs escape by the hair of their chinny chin chins
2-2 sounds a hell of a lot better than 1-3.

Apparently, Jay Cutler disagrees.

Here was the situation: Flying Pigs 767, Red Hornets 613; the Pigs had Cutler remaining, while the only bullet left in the Hornets’ chamber was its kicker, Stephen Gostkowski. QB vs. Kicker. QB with a 15.4-point cushion.

Shit, the fat lady needn’t have even warmed up her pipes; the curtain had already dropped. The only question at that point was the margin of the Pigs’ victory.

Well, if that was indeed the question, it’s highly doubtful anyone got the answer correct: 2. That’s right: Gostkowski outscored Cutler by 13.4 points. And that’s not because Gostkowski went off or anything. 11 is a respectable tally for a kicker, to be sure, but hardly enough to strike fear into the heart of an opponent with a 15.4 point lead and a quarterback in his back pocket.

His back pocket is where Jacob should have left Cutler.

The monumental douchebag (once you play in Denver, you’re forever a douche…also, Cutler really is a d-bag, by all accounts) was disastrous from the start. They say quarterbacks have to have a clock in their heads to time how long they have before the pass rush arrives. If that’s true, Cutler must’ve forgotten to wind his. He was sacked nine times…in the first half. He was sacked right past zero and into Negative Numbers Land. An interception and three fumbles helped pave the way into that dark abyss, as well.

But the Pigs caught a break just before their golden chariot transformed fully into a pumpkin: Cutler never came back into the game after halftime. Who knows how much more damage he could have done to Jacob’s squad? Luckily for Jake, the New York Giants did enough damage to Cutler’s head to keep the answer a mystery. Cutler did his best to yank his team down to 1-3, but as usual, his best wasn’t good enough (Vanderbilt called: they want to know where those bowl appearances Jay led them to are).

So the Pigs climb to 2-2, and a .500 mark four games into the season is more than satisfactory for the normally defunct Pigs. Now they just have to find a new QB for Week 5 and beyond, because Jay Cutler went “Wee! Wee! Wee!” all the way to the infirmary.
