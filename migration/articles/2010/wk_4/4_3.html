A JADDL first: midseason NFL trade
Randy Moss will have the same quarterback he’s had this entire season. Same running backs. Same wide receiver colleagues. Same owner.

Yet he won’t, at the same time.

As of Tuesday night, rumors exploded on the Interwebs about a possible trade—in the NFL, not the JADDL. This trade would send Randy Moss from the Patriots to the Vikings in exchange for a third round pick. By Wednesday morning, the Twittersphere made the trade sound imminent.

So, the cast of characters surrounding Moss IRL (“in real life” for you non-nerds—a.k.a. bad fantasy football owners) will change drastically. Gone will be the flowing golden-brown locks of Tom “Bangin’” Brady, and in will come the gray bearded, Wrangler-sportin’ Brett Favre, nicknamed the “Gunslinger” because he’s actually old enough to have starred in a few old westerns alongside John Wayne. Oh, that isn’t the reason? Well, it ought to be.

Also gone will be the so-so running game of New England, supplanted by the formidable ground attack of Minnesota, the 5th best rushing team in the NFL. Sure, “All Day” hasn’t been quite as dominant as years past, because Favre makes sure the show in town is all about Favre, but it’s still more than Moss had to put up with from the likes of…who is New England’s premiere back, anyway? Is Kevin Faulk still lurking around there these days?

While his NFL family undergoes a complete turnover, Moss’ fantastic family remains the same. When he dons the jet-black Boom unis, he’ll still be looking down the line at Joe Flacco under center (unless he gets swapped for flavor-of-the-month Kyle Orton, that is). He’ll still be competing for touches with Matt Forte, Malcolm Floyd, and Mike Williams.

And he’ll still have the comfort of being led into battle by one of the most unrelenting, ruthless, competitive sons of bitches to ever grace the gridiron.

Not Bill Belichick…

Peter Anderson.
