Family feud is all Bad News
In an opening week full of intriguing storylines, none are quite as cute as the in-law rivalry between the Bad News Bensons and the Millennium Falcons.

There’s just one problem: this isn’t a rivalry at all. At least not yet.

You see, the Bensons (formerly The Return of Ron Mexico) are 3-0 against the Falcons since Ian joined the league. What’s more: the gap is widening, not closing in. In 2009 the teams met twice, with Alex winning the first game by six (77-71) and the second by seven (84-77). With the 2010 season in its infancy, Alex went into full-on big brother beatdown mode, ripping Ian by 44, 125.3 - 81.2.

This is going to make for an awkward Thanksgiving.

Then again, because the Bensons and Falcons are in the same division, Ian will get another crack at his brother from another mother. That rematch comes in Week 9. A win would up Ian’s record against his sister’s husband to a still-awful 1-3, but hey…baby steps.

Alex’s wife / Ian’s sister

Speaking of Ian’s sister (and Alex’s wife), she’s the real victim here; let’s not lose sight of that. Every year, she’s forced to choose sides between the guy she’s related to by blood, and the guy she settled for in marriage. That has to be difficult, right?

“No,” she says. “I don’t pick sides beforehand. I just give the loser a bunch of shit, whomever that turns out to be.”

Sounds like Ian’s been taking a lot of shit from his big sister the past couple years. Someone might be in need of a hug.

As for the game itself, the Bensons took the first Briefly Badass award of the season with an impressively balanced attack. Cedric Benson was the only player who didn’t reach double figures. Damn. On the Falcons’ side, Ian’s going to need more than 14 points from his WR tandem, and certainly more than 8.4 from his QB, to compete for a playoff spot.
