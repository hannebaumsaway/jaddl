Longshanks fire trainer
A disturbing trend is forming for the Fightin’ Longshanks which has left them with a little less fight and a little long in the face. For the second straight year, the ’Shanks enter Week 3 of the regular season with a would-be starter on the long-term IR.

Last year, the ’Shanks took a chance on Indianapolis WR Anthony Gonzales, who was battling a nagging injury during the preseason but expected to be ready by the start of the regular season.

Eh, no.

Gonzales spent the entire year on sidelines, leisurely peddaling the stationary bike while he raked in stacks of cash for doing absolutely nothing; he didn’t play a single down the entire year. The Longshanks’ WR corps was subsequently left thin and scraping for points, and Ryan’s squad missed the playoffs for the first time in franchise history.

This year’s victim is Reggie Bush, who broke his fibula in Monday night’s game. Bush was the Longshanks’ 7th Round pick, the 79th pick overall, and Ryan had planned on playing him a significant number of weeks this season. Now, Bush is likely out at least six weeks, and when he returns, experts say he will probably have lost a step.

Fed up with this recurring issue, the Longshanks announced Wednesday that they terminated long-time head trainer Robert Bruce. Bruce had been with the team since the franchise’s inception.

“It saddens me,” said Ryan of the firing. “It saddens me that Robert turned out to be so terrible at his job. It saddens me that my high caliber athletes are at the mercy of a lazy fat-ass like him. It saddens me that I didn’t fire him last year.”

Bruce could not be reached for comment.
