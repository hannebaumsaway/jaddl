So Horny right now
“Come get some.”

That’s the message the Red Hornets are spreading to the league. “Remember us?” is another one. With The Mighty Boom continuing to march through its schedule unscathed, the picture is beginning to look eerily familiar. It was Team Odouls who poked the Hornets’ nest while undefeated a year ago, and the Hornets made Odouls its bitch—twice—en route to the JADDL crown.

Will history repeat? It’s certainly looking like the Hornets are worthy of challenging for the title yet again, as they buzzed to an easy 84.3–47.2 victory over The Fifth Reich this past weekend, moving them to third place in the league at 4-2.

It’s hard to say why people consistently overlook the Hornets. Some say it’s because they’re located clear the hell out in McCainland, also known as Arizona. Others think it’s due to their owner, Daniel, being a damn southpaw daywalker, and so hard to take seriously. Whatever the reason: it’s a bad idea.

“I enjoy playing the underdog role,” said Dan. “Opponents never took me and my ‘skyball’ seriously when we played Caps back in the day, either, and…well, I lost a lot of Caps games, so forget that comparison.”

The next few weeks will tell a lot about what kind of team the Hornets truly have, as they have to plow through the gauntlet of the Leavenworth Law Dogs, The Mighty Boom, and the Fightin’ Longshanks, the fourth-, first-, and fifth-place teams, respectively.

The Fifth Reich, on the other hand, is losing traction on its “scary underdog” role, losers of two straight after blitzing to an improbable .500 record. They’re starting to look more like the Reich we all laughed at during the draft.

“M—ssen wir diese Schei—e drehte sich um,” said Reich owner John.
