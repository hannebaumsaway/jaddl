These little Piggies went “wee! wee! wee!” all the way to the hizzouse…for what, exactly?
Yes, this was only their second win, and yes, The Flying Pigs are well out of playoff contention.

But that didn’t stop them from playing a part in shaping the playoff picture this past weekend. In a game that left the playoff participants and seeding even more in question than they already were, the Pigs handed the Leavenworth Law Dogs their third loss of the season in a nail-biter, 91.4–90.9.

The game came down to the final minutes of Monday night. Pigs RB Frank Gore provided the dagger that neutered the Dogs as he scampered for a mere 3 yards with 6:53 left to go in the game. Those 3 yards put the Pigs ahead of the Dogs by 0.2 points. Gore finished his game-winning day with 10.5 points.

“Going into the day, I knew what I needed to do for us to win,” said Gore. “I could’ve won a few other games for us, but since Ryan has money riding on—I just gave 110% tonight.”

Say what? The Pigs have lost 3 games this season by less than 10 points. In those 3 losses, Gore averaged 13.9 points—a respectable mark. Fellow RB Ryan Grant, however, averaged just 6.9 points in those losing efforts. When asked about the accusation and whether it’s caused trouble in the locker room, Pigs owner Jacob simply shook his head.

“Hey, at least someone on this team is having fun this year,” scoffed Jacob. “What last-place team doesn’t have trouble in the locker room? I hate most of these guys personally.”

The game, though hardly cheering up Pigs fans (all 17 of them), may have huge implications for the league. It dropped the Law Dogs to 7-3, a much more vulnerable spot seeding-wise than 8-2 would have been. Team Hauloll and the Fightin’ Longshanks, both victors this past weekend, are close enough to sniff the Dogs’ butt.
