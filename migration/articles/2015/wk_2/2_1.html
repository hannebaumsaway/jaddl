No Bell, no problem
DeAngelo Williams proves a worthy understudy
James knew he would have to survive these first two weeks of the season. He was playing the long game when he drafted Le’Veon Bell number one overall back in August, considering Bell’s two-game suspension. But James wasn’t deterred from drafting the guy he deemed the best player in the league. He had enough faith in his abilities as a general manager to trust he could craft a team around Bell which could weather the suspension.

James? Faith in his GM abilities? Really?

Yes, really. Over the first five years of the JADDL, Lawrence Football Jesus—or D.C. Football Obama, briefly—averaged 1,005 points per season. That was good, naturally, for dead last in the league. By a lot.

But from 2012 to 2014, Jesus upped that average to 1,175. That number is eighth best over that span (all four teams behind Jesus are from the West, coincidentally…ehhhrrrm). Is eighth best something to brag about? Maybe not, but it represents significant progress for a much-maligned franchise.

That franchise is looking to parlay the number one overall pick into another big step forward this year. James had the top pick once before, if you’ll recall: the infamous Michael Vick pick. But we’ll have to wait one more week to begin evaluating how this pick is going to turn out.

In the meantime, LFJ has been leaning on Bell’s backup, DeAngelo Williams. That’s right: the same DeAngelo Williams who tore the league up en route to a Jared Bowl II MVP performance…seven years ago. Williams is 32 years old now, but apparently he’s still got it. Instead of James counting down the days until Bell’s return, he’s been tallying up the points Williams has been piling up—40.4 of them, in total.

That includes 27.2 in his last outing against the Fightin’ Longshanks, a dominant 113.7–65.4 victory for Jesus. When Williams ran for 127 yards in Week 1 and scored 13 points, people acknowledged the good fortune for Jesus, despite an eventual loss. Now, people are actually discussing whether the Steelers—and James—should consider giving Williams the starting nod over Bell in Week 3, to ride the hot hand. I’m serious; people are actually debating that.

But James is not. Before the stadium lights had even cooled off late Monday night, James made the switch on his depth chart. It will be Le’Veon Bell trotting onto the field when LFJ takes on the Millennium Falcons. Bell will carry lofty expectations on his shoulders, those of a savior…but Football Jesus may not need “saving” after all. Their roster is balanced, as James himself observes in his DDL post.
I love this team. I’m just going to enjoy this.
James on The DDL

It’s balanced, and potent. Through the first two weeks, Football Jesus leads the entire league in points scored. They have a top five quarterback in Aaron Rodgers; a top five receiver in Julian Edelman; a top five tight end in Jason Witten (though we’ll have to see what the Tony Romo injury does to his production).

They also have the top running back in the league so far. And it’s not Le’Veon Bell.

…Yet.
