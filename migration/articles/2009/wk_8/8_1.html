MJD: little dude, huge…heart
Dan aims to become the first 3-time champion in JADDL history. He nabbed his first Limousine in 2004. The Fightin’ Longshanks also won two titles recently, winning in ’05 and ’08. One of the champions that split up the Longshanks’ crowns?

Romans’ Red Hornets.

The rosy little insects have got serious sting again in 2009, averaging 99.8 points per outing. That isn’t tops in the league—The Mighty Boom, Team Odouls, and Team Hauloll all score more (that’s what she said)—but it’s been enough to get the ’Nets to 7-1 and first place.

The Buzzing Gingers are led by RB Maurice Jones-Drew, who put up 33.3 points in a 104.9 — 85.5 trouncing of In Pursuit of Perfection, LLC this past weekend. It’s fitting Jones-Drew plays for the Hornets, as he’s bug-sized himself. But he’s shifty as hell, a staple on John Madden’s All Players-With-Three-Names Team. And though he may be small in stature, one of his body parts is not.

…His heart.

“Oh yeah, it’s freaking huge,” said Dan. “I go down to the locker room after some games, and I’ve seen it. His heart. It’s enormous.”

Teammates agree with Romans’ assessment of Jones-Drew’s most valuable organ.

“He’s like a tripod,” says Hornets QB Aaron “Not Brett Favre” Rodgers, “with the legs of the tripod being his two arms and his heart. I’m really jealous. I wish I could get my heart on like that.”

Reports are that someone else on the team is also jealous, to a level that could cause team dissention if not addressed soon.
It’s freaking huge. I go down to the locker room after some games, and I’ve seen it. His heart.
Dan, Red Hornets owner

“I don’t know why people think MJD’s is so big,” whined teammate Terrell Owens. “I’m all heart mother fucker! I gave a shorty my heart in a box last Christmas, and she loved it. Any of you punks question my heart, I’ll slap you ’cross the face with it.”

Barring any serious heart-slapping fights in the clubhouse, though, this Hornets team is very much in the hunt for the Golden Limousine at this point. Dan, a high profile member of the Daywalker community, knows what implications his personal success could have on the sun-adverse population.

“I want to show my fair-skinned brethren that though we may fear UV rays, and though society generally fears us, we can still achieve anything we put our minds to. Well, except a tan.”
