Never tell ’em the odds
You knew this was coming when the Millennium Falcons were featured: an article littered with Star Wars quotes.

Uh, everything is under control. Situation normal. Despite starting out 1-4, the Falcons have righted the ship (which will make point-five past light speed) and won their last two games by an average of 31 points, scoring in triple figures both times out. Suddenly, they’re just one game shy of .500 and within reach of a coveted top six slot.

That’s great, kid! Don’t get cocky. That being said, the Falcons still have an asteroid field to weave through, as four of their final six opponents have winning records. Falcons WR Chad Ochocinco doesn’t seem concerned. Since being acquired by owner Ian Wolfe, Ochocinco has fully embraced his inner geek to support the franchise.
I’m not saying “kiss the baby” anymore. I’m saying “kiss the Wookiee.”
Falcons WR Chad Ochocinco

“I’m not saying ‘kiss the baby’ anymore,” declared Ochocinco. “I’m saying ‘kiss the Wookiee’ instead.”

She’s the ship that made the Kessel Run in less than 12 parsecs. She’s fast enough for ya, old man. The road ahead may be daunting, but the Falcons are winning with more than just smoke and mirrors. They’ve averaged 117.4 points the past two weeks, even taking home the Briefly Badass award this past weekend for the league’s highest score. They’re led by a speedy trio of deep-threat receivers: Marques Colston, Donald Driver, and Ochocinco, all of whom average double figures. Wolfe also has two 18-point-average QBs at his disposal in Phillip Rivers and Joe Flacco.

Hokey religions and ancient weapons are no match for a good blaster at your side, kid. The ancient weapon in this case was wielded by the Falcons’ latest victim, the Fightin’ Longshanks: LaDainian Tomlinson. The ’Shanks are in full-fledged implosion mode, having dropped their last five games, most in embarrassing fashion; the latest, of course, a 119.6 — 88.5 drubbing at the hands of the Falcons.

She’ll hold together. Hear me baby? Hold together. It will be a crucial next few weeks for the boys from a galaxy far, far away. Take down 4-3 Team Hauloll in Week 8, and they may leap them in the standings. Lose and drop to 3-5, and the margin for error becomes zero.

You cannot escape your destiny. Or can they? The destiny for first-year franchises in the JADDL has been total and complete suckage. A playoff appearance would certainly buck that trend. But can the Falcons do it? Or will there be always with them that which cannot be done? We’ll have to wait for the answer, because…

Difficult to see the future is.
